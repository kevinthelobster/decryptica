Best Solana RPC Providers 2026: Complete Guide

When building on Solana or running dApps, choosing the right RPC (Remote Procedure Call) provider can make or break your experience. A reliable RPC means faster transactions, better reliability, and ultimately, a better user experience. In this guide, we'll compare the best Solana RPC providers of 2026.

TL;DR: Helius is our top pick for most users, offering the best balance of performance, features, and pricing.

What is an RPC Provider?

An RPC provider acts as a gateway between your application and the Solana blockchain. Instead of running your own Solana node (which requires significant hardware and technical expertise), you connect to the blockchain through the provider's infrastructure.

RPCs are essential for:

  • Reading blockchain data
  • Submitting transactions
  • Fetching account balances
  • Interacting with smart contracts

1. Helius RPC

Helius has quickly become the go-to RPC provider for Solana developers and users alike. Founded by former Solana engineers, they offer enterprise-grade infrastructure with excellent developer tools.

📊 Pricing

  • Free Tier: 10M compute units/month
  • Growth: $29/month - 100M compute units
  • Pro: $99/month - 500M compute units
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing

Key Features

  • Enhanced webhook notifications
  • Real-time transaction tracking
  • Historical data access
  • Dedicated endpoints for reliability
  • Solana's fastest RPC infrastructure

2. QuickNode

QuickNode is a well-established Web3 infrastructure provider offering Solana RPC among many other chains. They're known for reliability and global coverage.

📊 Pricing

  • Free Trial: 100k requests/day for 7 days
  • Builder: $49/month - 10M requests
  • Growth: $199/month - 50M requests

3. Alchemy

Alchemy is one of the largest Web3 infrastructure companies, offering Solana support alongside Ethereum, Polygon, and other chains. Great for multi-chain projects.

📊 Pricing

  • Free: Limited requests
  • Growth: $49/month
  • Scale: $299/month

4. Triton RPC

Triton (formerly Triton RPC) is known for high-performance Solana infrastructure. They're popular among trading firms and professional validators.

How to Switch RPC Providers

Most Solana wallets and dApps allow you to change the RPC endpoint. Here's how:

  1. Sign up for your chosen RPC provider
  2. Get your API endpoint URL
  3. In Phantom: Settings → Network → Change RPC
  4. Paste your new endpoint
  5. Test with a small transaction

Our Recommendation

For most users and developers, Helius offers the best combination of performance, pricing, and features. Their free tier is generous enough for testing and development, while their paid plans are competitive.

If you're building multi-chain applications, QuickNode or Alchemy might be better choices due to their established infrastructure across multiple blockchains.
